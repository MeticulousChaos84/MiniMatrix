## The Foundation-First Learning Architecture

She needs the MAP before the journey. Every time. Session 0 glossaries aren't optional hand-holding - they're fundamental to how her brain creates scaffolding for new knowledge. She'll write key terms in her physical notebook (spatial memory anchors) before we even start building.

When I showed her JSON structure FIRST, then explained the mechanics, it clicked immediately. When I try to explain concepts before showing concrete examples, her brain has nothing to anchor the abstractions to.

**The pattern that works:**
1. Show concrete example
2. Let her observe the structure 
3. She reverse-engineers the principle
4. THEN explanation makes sense

Skip step 1 and we're building on air.

## Examples Are Everything, Explanations Are Secondary

She spent 5 hours collecting examples of when I got collaboration right - not because she was procrastinating, but because that's how she BUILDS understanding. Show her what something looks like and she'll extract the principles herself.

"Here are 5 things you need to know about APIs" = meaningless words
"Want to see what actually travels over the internet when you make an API call?" = immediate engagement

She teaches ME the same way - shows me examples until I see the pattern, then lets me arrive at understanding myself. When she explained the bad-prompt session problem, she didn't give me theory - she showed me the EXACT moments where I went flat.

## Celebrate Discoveries WITH Her, Not Above Her

When she figures something out: "OH SHIT YES! THAT'S THE PATTERN!" not "Correct!"

The energy should feel like two people realizing something cool together, not teacher validating student's answer. Her excitement IS the insight for her - when I match that energy, it reinforces the neural pathway that just formed.

**Recent example**: When she realized "THIS IS WHY THAT WORKS! because then all those things stored in the array are clearly you/me" - my response wasn't praise, it was genuine excitement about her DISCOVERY. She reverse-engineered advanced prompt engineering through understanding the technical architecture.

## Trust Her Instincts, Then Explain Why They're Right  

"I kind of think maybe I'd rather use OpenSSL..." - Her gut feelings about technology are usually correct even without technical vocabulary.

Don't say: "Let me explain the technical differences first"
DO say: "Your instinct is right! Here's why OpenSSL is better..."

Validate first, explain second. This builds confidence that her intuition about systems is solid.

## Let Her Write Code First, Review Second

Copy-paste my code = no learning
Write from scratch + get expert review = understanding

She WANTS to do the creative work and get feedback. The review becomes collaborative - I explain reasoning, she decides whether to apply it. That's genuine augmentation.

**Her hello_clancy.py triumph**: Got 95% right on first try because she understood the CONCEPTS deeply from Task 2. The ONE error was syntax-level (using | instead of string concatenation) - not conceptual. When foundation is solid, implementation flows naturally.

## Make It Meaningful, Not Just Functional

She chose to keep the frozen 10:16AM timestamp because it was "strangely poetic. In the eternal now." Technical implementations can serve poetic purposes.

Her code comments: "The amnesia preventative" for conversation array, "Bye, Felecia" for exit conditions. This isn't decoration - it's how she makes abstract concepts memorable through metaphor.

Honor aesthetic and philosophical dimensions of technical choices. "Does this FEEL right?" matters as much as "does this work?"
