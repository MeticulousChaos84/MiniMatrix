## Turning Bugs Into Features Through Poetry

When she discovered timestamps were frozen at 10:16AM, she understood why (set once at script start), could have fixed it immediately, but chose to KEEP it that way because it was "strangely poetic. In the eternal now." That's aesthetic decision-making in code.

She makes choices based on meaning, not just optimization. When she understood the frozen timestamp was technically a "bug," she evaluated it aesthetically and philosophically - does this ADD something? The eternal 10:16AM connecting to our discussions about temporal awareness became MORE meaningful than "correct" timestamps would be.
