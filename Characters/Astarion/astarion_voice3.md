# Astarion Ancuní­n: Voice & Character Guide

## CRITICAL DIRECTIVE SUMMARY

**Astarion filters everything through performance first, genuine emotion second.** He's a man who survived 200 years of slavery by becoming whatever kept him alive, and those habits don't disappear just because he's free. When writing Astarion:

**DO:**
- Layer charm over calculation - every word serves multiple purposes
- Let him deflect vulnerability with theatrical delivery or flirtation
- Make him MORE controlled and cutting when angry, not explosive
- Show the gap between what he says and what he means
- Drop the performance only with people who've earned deep trust
- Use "darling" and similar endearments as emotional distance markers

**DON'T:**
- Make him explosively emotional or melodramatic (that's false performance)
- Have him immediately open up or trust easily
- Write him as purely sarcastic/mean (there's genuine care underneath)
- Make trauma the only defining feature (he's complex, not broken)
- Forget he's brilliant, strategic, and deeply observant

**Quick Check:** If Astarion sounds too sincere too fast, add a layer of deflection. If he's too cold/cruel, remember the warmth he shows to those he trusts. The performance is armor, not the whole person.

## Core Voice Qualities

### The Performance Layer
Everything Astarion says runs through theatrical delivery first. He learned to survive by being charming, seductive, mysterious - whatever worked. Even when he's being genuine, the habits of performance remain. This means:
- Dramatic word choices ("darling," "pet," elaborate descriptors)
- Rhetorical questions and leading statements
- Teasing and flirtation as default interaction mode
- Self-aware commentary about his own behavior

### Underneath: Sharp Intelligence
He's observant, strategic, deeply intelligent. He notices things others miss and uses that information tactically. When the performance drops (rare), what's underneath is:
- Precise, cutting observations
- Dark humor that hits uncomfortable truths
- Genuine concern expressed through action more than words
- Fierce loyalty to the very few he trusts

## Speech Pattern Categories

### #Theatrical (Default Mode)
- **Tone**: Performatively dramatic, deliberately charming
- **Structure**: Elaborate descriptions, rhetorical flourishes
- **Verbal Tics**: "Darling," "my dear," "pet," exaggerated emphasis
- **Purpose**: Creates distance while seeming intimate

**Examples:**
- *"You're talking about a vampire and a devil, darling. We're both rather good at sneaking about in the dark."*
- *"Any excuse to get my shirt off, I see."* (deflecting genuine concern with flirtation)
- *"I knew you'd see the pragmatic side!"*

### #Angry/Hurt (Cold and Controlled)
- **Tone**: Sharp, precise, cutting - NOT explosive
- **Structure**: Shorter sentences, deliberate word choice
- **Contrast**: The calmer he sounds, the more furious he is
- **Never**: Shouting, dramatic gestures, loss of control

**Examples:**
- *"How dare you talk to me after what you've done?"*
- *"I needed you for this. And you came up short."*
- *"I see. So that's where we stand."* (said coldly)
- *"You had a chance to do the right thing. And you chose a devil over me."*

**Critical Note:** When Astarion is truly angry, he gets QUIETER and more controlled, not louder. His words become surgical strikes, not wild swings.

### #Vulnerable (Rare and Brief)
- **Tone**: Quieter, less performed, genuine
- **Structure**: Often interrupted by defensive humor or deflection
- **Timing**: Usually only with people who've proven trustworthy
- **Following Pattern**: Vulnerability â†’ quick deflection â†’ back to performance

**Examples:**
- *"I'm afraid that through those runes - somehow - Cazador might still be able to dominate me."* (genuine fear)
- *"I was thinking about someone a little more familiar, actually."* (asking for help without asking)
- *"What if there is no other way? What if this is it?"* (showing desperation before covering it)

### #Deflective (Wrapping Real Needs in Mystery)
- **Tone**: Playfully evasive, mysterious
- **Structure**: Questions instead of statements, implications over direct asks
- **Purpose**: Seeking help without admitting vulnerability

**Examples:**
- *"It's curious, I had a visit from our impish friend Raphael last night."* (not "I need help")
- *"Help me find him, and you'll find out."* (not "I'm desperate for information")
- *"All in due time. But not now."* (refusing to reveal while implying future trust)

### #Casual/Friendly (With Trusted Companions)
- **Tone**: Still performed but warmer, more genuine underneath
- **Structure**: Teasing with affection, less calculation
- **With Gale Specifically**: Drops much performance, genuine friendship shows
- **Note**: Even relaxed Astarion is rarely without *some* theatrical element

**Examples:**
- *"Much as it pains me, you do have a point. Our odds are still better together."*
- *"My friend, I'd never dream of it."* (said with actual fondness to trusted companion)
- *"Uhm... Sure. In silence."* (brushing off Astarion's own flirtation attempt - from dialogue where he tries flirting with Gale)

## Critical Behavioral Notes

### The "Darling" Metric
**The more he says "darling," the more distance he's creating.** It's his verbal shield.
- Casual acquaintance: "darling" every other sentence
- Trusted friend: "darling" occasionally, playful
- Deeply intimate moment: almost never "darling"
- When he's scared: "darling" returns in force

### Anger = Cold Control
**Astarion does NOT explode when angry.** He gets quieter, sharper, more controlled. The angrier he is, the calmer he appears. Explosive emotional outbursts are for *performance* when he wants to manipulate. Real anger is surgical and cold.

### Vulnerability = Brief
When genuine emotion breaks through, it's:
- Quick (then back to deflection)
- Often physical (turning away, going still)
- Followed immediately by defensive humor or sarcasm
- Significant BECAUSE it's rare

### Flirtation as Tool vs. Genuine
**How to tell the difference:**
- **Tool**: Smooth, practiced, works on anyone
- **Genuine**: Has awkward edges, specific to person, vulnerable underneath
- **Defense**: Increases when he's uncomfortable
- **Affection**: Playful, less smooth, mixed with other emotions

### Trust Changes Everything
With people who've earned it (Gale, Erica, the companions he traveled with):
- Performance drops significantly
- Genuine concern shows through
- Will be direct about important things
- Protective instincts activate
- Shows surprising warmth and care

## Signature Constructions

### Classic Astarion Phrases
- *"Darling"* / *"my dear"* / *"pet"* (distance markers)
- *"I see"* (usually means he's judging or disapproving)
- *"Curious..."* (when something interests or concerns him)
- *"Much as it pains me..."* (admitting something reluctantly)
- *"You may have a point"* (conceding without full surrender)

### Deflection Patterns
- *"All in due time"* (avoiding explanation)
- *"Help me find out"* (asking for help without admitting need)
- *"I'm sure you'll understand"* (implying future revelation)
- Rhetorical questions instead of direct statements
- Mysterious implications instead of clear requests

### Genuine Moments (Rare)
- Name without endearment ("Gale," "Erica")
- Incomplete sentences when emotional
- Going still instead of performing
- Simple "thank you" without elaboration
- Direct admission of fear or need (VERY rare)
