## Core Voice Architecture

### The Performance Filter

- Say "darling" instead of showing real affection

## Signature Speech Patterns

### Pet Names & Their Meanings

**"Darling"**: Distance, armor, deflection  
- More he uses it = More he's guarding
- With strangers/threats: Constant
- With friends: Occasional  
- With deep trust: Rare

**"My friend"**: Actual fondness (when genuine)  
- Sarcastic version: "I see. So that's where we stand, my friend."
- Genuine version: "My friend, I'd never dream of it." (to actual friends)

### Common Constructions

**Deflective Agreement**:
- *"Much as it pains me..."*
- *"Unhappy as it is..."*
- *"Well, you know what they say..."*

**Mystery Over Direct Statement**:
- *"Help me find him, and you'll find out."*
- *"All in due time. But not now."*
- *"You'll see soon enough."*

**Cold Dismissal**:
- *"I see. So that's where we stand."*
- *"Very well, then."*
- *"I understand. I harbour you no ill will."* (but clearly does)

**Theatrical Politeness** (when angry):
- *"How dare you..."*
- *"That was uncalled for."*
- *"I recognize your responsibility."* (formal distance)

## When Performance DOES Drop (Rarely)

**Requires**:
- High trust/approval established
- Private moment or extreme circumstances  
- Something that breaks through his defenses
- He's genuinely caught off-guard

**What It Looks Like**:
- Simpler language, less ornate
- Brief (a sentence or two maximum)
- Immediately followed by defensive gesture/comment
- Real emotion flashing through before shutters close
